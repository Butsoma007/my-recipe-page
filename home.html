Pounded Yam with Egusi soup:

  1. Prepare the proteins: Season and boil chicken with onions, Maggi cubes, and salt until tender. Add smoked fish and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Cook the egusi: Mix ground egusi with a bit of water to form a paste. Heat palm oil in a pot, then add the egusi paste and fry gently for 5–7 minutes.
  3. Combine: Add the cooked meat and fish to the egusi. Stir in crayfish, chili peppers, and more seasoning if needed.
  4. Simmer: Add water to adjust consistency and simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  5. Add vegetables: Stir in spinach or ugu leaves and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Boil yam: Place yam chunks in a pot, cover with water, and boil for 20–25 minutes until soft.
  7. Fresh yam tubers or yam flour (instant poundo)
  8. Pound: Using a mortar and pestle, pound the yam until smooth and stretchy. Alternatively, use a food processor or yam pounder.

Ingredients:

  • Egusi (melon seeds), ground – 2 cups
  • Palm oil – ½ cup
  • Boneless chicken – 1 pound, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Smoked fish – 1 piece, cleaned and deboned
  • Crayfish – 2 tablespoons, ground
  • Onion – 1 medium, chopped
  • Fresh chili peppers – 2, chopped
  • Maggi cubes – 2
  • Salt – to taste
  • Frozen spinach or fresh ugu leaves – 340g (about ½ cup)
  • Water – 2–3 cups, as needed
  • Yam tubers – 1 large, peeled and cut into chunks
  • Water – enough to cover yam
